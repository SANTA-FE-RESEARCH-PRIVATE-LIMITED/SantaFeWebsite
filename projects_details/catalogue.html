Intelligent Tagging For Enriching Catalogue

Task

Develop a serverless pipeline to detect category, and attributes from images in a fashion retailer's catalogue.

Client

A deep-tech start-up that assists fashion retailers to customise shopping experience by offering products like collections generation, inventory optimisation, and shopper persona mapping.

Business Challenge

The in-house fashion experts have crafted a standardised taxonomy to organise fashion retailer's catalogue : Each product is allocated to a distinct category, and assigned category-specific attributes.

Presently, annotators process 100 images per hour using this taxonomy. The objective is to develop a serverless pipeline that boosts the throughput to 10,000 images per hour while adhering to the standardised taxonomy. .

Disruption Delivered

A serverless pipeline that can process up to 15000 images per hour. Pipeline is a DAG that implements category detection, and attribute classification based on the standardised taxonomy. The dependent downstream processes can subscribe to the pipeline, and operate on the enriched data.

The solution has not only secured 2 US patents for the client but has also been integrated as the first stage of their flagship product. Currently, the product has served 1 Million + customers, and increased conversions to retailers by 50%. .

Solution Overview

We created an object detection pipeline to recognise categories from an image. Our classification model then assigns specific "attributes" to the image based on the detected category.

This structured approach allows us to identify multiple categories in a single image, and associate them with the corresponding attributes from our taxonomy.



Architecture Overview

User Interface Layer

We developed a streamlit app for rapid prototyping. The UI layer helps us to trigger the pipeline by uploading a valid payload to the data registry.



Preprocessing Layer

The images in the catalogue might not be valid, and downloadable. Additionally, they need to standardised for consumption by the inference layer. The preprocessing layers performs this task.



Inference Layer

The inference layer identifies the categories from the pre-processed images. Based on the categories identified, the images are consumed by category specific multi-label classifier to generate the "attributes".



  • Reporting Layer

    The status of failed images, and data from inference layer are processed to create a report. This report will be consumed by the end-user, and downstream.



  • Dash-boarding Layer

    The dash-boarding layer consumes the payload, and reports to track the status of tasks, distribution of categories, and distribution of attributes.
